What is Mental Health?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act every day.

Warning Signs

  • Feeling sad or hopeless
  • Withdrawing from friends and family
  • Extreme mood changes
  • Difficulty sleeping or sleeping too much
  • Loss of interest in activities you once enjoyed

Get Help

If you or someone you know is struggling, reach out:

  • National Crisis Hotline: Call or text 988
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
  • NAMI Helpline: 1-800-950-6264

How to Help a Friend

If someone you care about is struggling with their mental health, here's how you can support them:

  • 💬 Listen without judgment — Just being there means a lot
  • ❤️ Show you care — Check in on them regularly
  • 🚫 Don't minimize their feelings — Avoid saying "just cheer up"
  • 📚 Learn about their condition — Understanding helps you support better
  • 🆘 Encourage professional help — Suggest therapy or counseling
  • 🤝 Be patient — Recovery takes time

Self Care Tips

Taking care of your mental health every day is important. Here are some simple tips:

  • 🌙 Get enough sleep every night (7-9 hours)
  • 🏃 Exercise regularly — even a short walk helps
  • 🥗 Eat healthy and stay hydrated
  • 📵 Limit social media and screen time
  • 🧘 Practice mindfulness or meditation
  • 📖 Journal your thoughts and feelings
  • 👫 Stay connected with friends and family
  • 🎨 Do things you enjoy and that bring you joy

Myths vs Facts

There are many misconceptions about mental health. Let's clear them up:

  • ❌ Myth: Mental health problems are a sign of weakness
    ✅ Fact: Mental health conditions are medical conditions, not character flaws

  • ❌ Myth: You can just "snap out of it"
    ✅ Fact: Mental health conditions require proper treatment and support

  • ❌ Myth: Therapy is only for "crazy" people
    ✅ Fact: Therapy helps anyone who wants to improve their mental well-being

  • ❌ Myth: Mental health problems don't affect young people
    ✅ Fact: 50% of mental health conditions begin by age 14

Types of Mental Health Conditions

There are many different types of mental health conditions. Here are some of the most common:

  • Anxiety Disorders — Excessive fear or worry that interferes with daily life
  • Depression — Persistent feelings of sadness, emptiness, or hopelessness
  • PTSD — Triggered by traumatic events, causing flashbacks and severe anxiety
  • Bipolar Disorder — Extreme mood swings between highs and lows
  • OCD — Unwanted repetitive thoughts or behaviors
  • Eating Disorders — Unhealthy relationships with food and body image
